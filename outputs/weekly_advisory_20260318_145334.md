**1) Quick Summary (5‑7 lines)**  
- Mid‑March in Kashmir is warm (≈27 °C) with occasional light rain; humidity is low, so soil dries quickly.  
- Soil temperature is ~25 °C but moisture is only 0.10 m³ m⁻³ – a dry seedbed for rice.  
- No irrigation infrastructure → rely on rain‑water capture, bunds, and mulching to keep the seedbed moist.  
- Beginner farmer on 40 ac of rain‑fed rice: start with land prep, broadcast sowing, and a modest basal fertilizer (Urea + DAP).  
- Key risks this week: unexpected heavy showers that can wash seedlings, rapid drying leading to poor germination, and early blast disease if leaf wetness stays high.

**4) Irrigation Strategy (no formal irrigation)**  

1. **Rain‑water harvesting** – the shallow furrows (Day 2) act as micro‑catchments; fill them during any rain and let water infiltrate slowly.  
2. **Mulch blanket** – straw mulch (Day 4) reduces evaporation by ~30‑40 % and keeps the seed zone moist.  
3. **Field‑level water budgeting** – aim for a minimum of 5 mm of soil‑available water after each rain event; if < 5 mm, create a small “ponded” area (≈0.5 ac) to hold runoff for later use.  
4. **Conservation tillage** – limit secondary tillage after Day 4 to preserve soil structure and moisture.

| Nutrient | Recommended Rate (40 ac) | Timing | Reason |

| **Potassium (K₂O)** | 120 kg (Muriate of Potash) | Apply at tillering (Week 3) | Supports stress tolerance (heat & disease). |
| **Organic matter** | 5 t FYM (Day 2) + 2 t compost (mid‑season) | Improves water‑holding capacity, especially important with low native moisture (0.10). |
| **Lime** (if pH < 6.0, test in Week 2) | 1 t/acre | Optional, before final leveling | Prevents Al toxicity, improves nutrient availability. |

**6) Contingency Plan**

| Situation | Trigger | Immediate Action |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| **Heavy rain (>10 mm in 24 h)** | Forecast or observed > 10 mm | • Open bund overflow channels to avoid waterlogging.<br>• Gently re‑level low spots after water recedes.<br>• Protect seedlings with a light thatch cover if possible. |
| **Prolonged dry spell (>5 days, no rain)** | Soil moisture probe < 0.05 m³ m⁻³ | • Mobilize stored rainwater (if any) to wet furrows.<br>• Increase mulch thickness (add extra straw).<br>• Reduce weed pressure aggressively (manual removal). |
| **Heat wave (max > 35 °C)** | Forecast or observed > 35 °C | • Shade young seedlings with temporary shade net (2 × 2 m frames).<br>• Apply a light foliar spray of water with a few drops of neem oil (pest control + cooling). |
| **Blast disease outbreak** | > 5 % leaves showing typical lesions | • Apply Trichoderma bio‑fungicide (Day 6 or as soon as detected).<br>• Remove heavily infected tillers to reduce inoculum.<br>• Ensure good field aeration (avoid water stagnation). |

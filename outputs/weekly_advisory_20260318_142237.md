**1) Quick Summary**  
- Chennai’s hot (32 °C) and humid (59 %) early‑March weather calls for steady water depth and heat‑stress monitoring.  
- Soil moisture is low (0.11 m³ m⁻³) – start with a flood and keep the field continuously wet for the first 2 weeks.  
- For 2 ac (~0.81 ha) of rice, basal N‑P‑K = 20 kg N + 12 kg P₂O₅ + 12 kg K₂O; split‑apply the remaining N (28 kg) at tillering & panicle‑initiation.  
- Beginner farmer – keep tasks simple, use visual checks, and record daily observations.  
- Main risks this week: heat spikes (>35 °C), sudden down‑pour (rare now), and early‑stage pests (leaf‑hoppers, blast).

**4) Irrigation Strategy (Irrigation = yes)**  

| Stage | Target Water Depth | Frequency | Method | Reason |
|-------|-------------------|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Establishment (Day 1‑7)** | 5‑10 cm (initial flood 10 cm for 24 h) | Daily check; add 2‑3 cm each morning if depth < 5 cm | Pumped flood from borewell/tank | Guarantees seedling establishment & compensates for high ET (≈ 5 mm day⁻¹) |
| **Tillering (Day 8‑21)** | 5‑7 cm | Every 2 days (or when soil moisture < 0.18 m³ m⁻³) | Flood & let water recede (intermittent) | Saves water, prevents anaerobic conditions, still meets crop water demand |
| **Panicle Initiation (after Day 21)** | 3‑5 cm (mid‑season drainage) | 3‑4 days interval, adopt **AWD** (Alternate Wetting & Drying) | Open sluice, allow field to dry to 15 cm below surface before re‑flooding | Reduces water use, improves root aeration, lowers methane emissions |

*Key tip for a beginner*: Use a simple wooden stake with a marked ruler to measure water depth at the field edge each morning.

**5) Fertility & Soil Management Plan**  

1. **Basal (Day 1)** – 20 kg N, 12 kg P₂O₅, 12 kg K₂O (as described).  
2. **Tillering (Day 4)** – 8 kg N + 4 kg P₂O₅ (split N).  
3. **Panicle Initiation (≈ Day 20)** – remaining 20 kg N (apply as urea dissolved in irrigation water).  
4. **Micronutrients** – If soil test later shows Zn deficiency (common in coastal soils), apply ZnSO₄ @ 5 kg ha⁻¹ (≈ 4 g m⁻²) as a foliar spray at tillering.  
5. **Organic matter** – Incorporate 2 t ha⁻¹ (≈ 1.6 t for 2 ac) of well‑decomposed farmyard manure (FYM) during Day 1 field preparation to improve moisture‑holding capacity.  
6. **pH Management** – Chennai soils are often slightly alkaline; if lab shows pH > 7.5, apply elemental sulfur 1 t ha⁻¹ (≈ 0.8 t) in the next fallow cycle – not needed this week.

**6) Contingency Plan**  

| Event | Immediate Action | Follow‑up |
|-------|------------------|----------|
| **Unexpected rain (>5 mm)** | Open field sluice gates, allow excess water to drain to 3‑5 cm depth within 2 hrs. | After rain stops, check for water‑logging signs (yellowing, root rot). If present, aerate by shallow tillage. |
| **Heat wave (max > 35 °C for >2 days)** | Raise water depth to 10 cm, irrigate early (5‑6 am) and late (5‑6 pm) to cool canopy. | Consider temporary shade net (shade factor 30 %) over 10 % of the field if temperatures exceed 38 °C. |
| **Water shortage (pump failure / low canal flow)** | Switch to **AWD** early (after Day 21) – let field dry to 15 cm below surface, then re‑flood. | Prioritize water for youngest seedlings; postpone non‑essential

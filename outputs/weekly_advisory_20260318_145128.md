**5) Fertility & Soil Management Plan**  

1. **Baseline** – Apply basal NPK (Day 1) as per recommended rates for 40 ac (≈ 480 kg N, 240 kg P₂O₅, 480 kg K₂O).  
2. **Nitrogen Splits** – 30 % basal (Day 1), 35 % at 15 days (Day 4), 35 % at 30 days (Day 7). This reduces blast risk linked to excess early N.  
3. **Phosphorus & Potassium** – No further P/K needed unless soil test (Day 6) shows deficiency; if pH < 5.5, apply **lime** (2 t/ha) in the next off‑season.  
4. **Organic Matter** – Broadcast **farmyard manure** (5 t/ha) after basal fertilizer (Day 1) to improve water‑holding capacity; incorporate lightly.  
5. **Micronutrients** – If soil test shows Zn deficiency (common in Kerala), apply ZnSO₄ (20 kg/ha) as a foliar spray at tillering (Day 5).  

*Why?* The early split‑N schedule matches the rapid vegetative growth of paddy while limiting the lush canopy that favors blast. Organic matter boosts the low moisture reading (0.228) by improving capillary water retention.

**6) Contingency Plan**  

| Event | Trigger | Immediate Action | Follow‑up |
|-------|---------|------------------|----------|
| **Unexpected heavy rain (≥ 15 mm in 24 h)** | Rain gauge or forecast | Close irrigation gates; drain excess water to keep depth ≤ 8 cm.<br>Inspect bunds for breach. | After rain, re‑measure soil moisture; if > 35 % skip next scheduled irrigation. |
| **Heat wave (max > 35 °C for ≥ 3 days)** | Weather alert | Increase water depth to 7‑8 cm; apply light shade (straw mulch) over seedlings.<br>Reduce nitrogen top‑dress by 20 % to avoid excessive vegetative growth. | Monitor for leaf scorch; if > 10 % plants show wilting, add 1 cm extra irrigation. |
| **Water shortage (pump failure or canal cut‑off)** | No water for > 24 h | Use stored tank water to maintain minimum 4 cm depth.<br>Prioritize critical stages (transplant, tillering). | Arrange repair; if shortage > 48 h, consider temporary dry‑seeding of a 5 ac buffer for later re‑planting. |
| **Blast outbreak** | > 5 % leaf area with typical lesions | Spray copper oxychloride (0.2 kg/ha) + carbendazim (if permitted) immediately.<br>Reduce N application for the next split by 30 %. | Re‑scout after 5 days; if disease spreads, consider systemic fungicide under extension guidance. |
